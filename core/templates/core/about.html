What is a Sangoma?

A Sangoma is a traditional healer in Nguni Southern African culture who is believed to be a conduit between the world of the living and the world of the ancestors (Amadlozi). They are consulted by those who are sick, by those who seek direction, and by those who feel spiritually unaligned.

Unlike izinyanga (herbalists), a Sangoma's primary role is divination — using tools such as bones, shells, coins, and other sacred objects to communicate with ancestral spirits. This process is known as ukubhula (throwing bones) and is a deeply respected spiritual practice across South Africa, Zimbabwe, Mozambique, and Swaziland.

The training to become a Sangoma, known as Ukuthwasa, is rigorous and spiritually demanding. It requires living under the guidance of a senior Sangoma and can last several years. It is only complete once the ancestors confirm readiness through ceremony and sign.
